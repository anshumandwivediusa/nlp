# Introduction

Natural Language Processing (NLP) is the computational study of human language, enabling machines to understand, interpret, and generate text and speech. It is a core subfield of computer science and is closely associated with artificial intelligence, as it bridges communication between humans and machines.

NLP draws upon multiple disciplines:

  - **Computational Linguistics** — applying algorithms to linguistic theory.
  - Information retrieval — extracting relevant knowledge from large text corpora.
  - Knowledge representation — encoding meaning and context for reasoning.
  - Linguistics — the study of syntax, semantics, and pragmatics that underpins language models.

At its core, NLP transforms unstructured natural language into structured, machine-readable data. This enables applications ranging from search engines and chatbots to machine translation and question answering.
