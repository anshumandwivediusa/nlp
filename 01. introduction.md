## Overview

Natural Language Processing (NLP) is a branch of Artificial Intelligence that enables computers to understand, interpret, and generate human language in both text and speech. It combines Linguistics, Statistical Methods, and Machine Learning to bridge communication between humans and machines.

_Note: It makes the Systems deal with Natural Language Processing using **Linguistic Theory**, **Statistical Methods**, **Deep Learning** and **Hybrid Approaches**._

## Conceptual Foundations of NLP
- **Linguistic Theory**: NLP draws from syntax (structure), semantics (meaning), and pragmatics (context). Early research was heavily rule-based, relying on formal grammars and symbolic logic.
- **Statistical Methods**: The shift in the 1990s brought probabilistic models (like Hidden Markov Models) for tasks such as speech recognition and part-of-speech tagging.
- **Deep Learning**: Modern NLP relies on neural architectures (RNNs, CNNs, Transformers) that learn hierarchical representations of language.
- **Hybrid Approaches**: Current research often blends symbolic reasoning with neural methods to balance interpretability and performance.

At its core, NLP transforms unstructured natural language into structured, machine-readable data. This enables applications ranging from search engines and chatbots to machine translation and question answering.

NLP combines techniques from computational linguistics, machine learning, deep learning, and linguistics to bridge the communication gap between humans and computers. Its goal is to transform unstructured natural language into structured, machine-readable representations, allowing intelligent systems to perform tasks such as text classification, information extraction, machine translation, question answering, summarization, and conversational AI.

Modern NLP is broadly divided into two closely related subfields:

 - **Natural Language Understanding (NLU)** – Focuses on enabling machines to **comprehend and interpret human language**. This includes tasks such as syntactic analysis, semantic analysis, sentiment analysis, named entity recognition, intent detection, and question answering.
 - **Natural Language Generation (NLG)** – Focuses on enabling machines to generate coherent, contextually appropriate human language. Common applications include text summarization, machine translation, dialogue systems, report generation, and **Large Language Models** (LLMs).

Although closely related, NLP is distinct from Speech Processing. Speech Processing deals with converting spoken language into text (Automatic Speech Recognition – ASR) and converting text into natural-sounding speech (Text-to-Speech – TTS). In many real-world AI systems, speech technologies and NLP work together—for example, in virtual assistants, voice-enabled chatbots, and transcription services.

Today, NLP forms the foundation of many intelligent applications, including search engines, chatbots, recommendation systems, sentiment analysis tools, Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) systems, and Large Language Models (LLMs) such as GPT, BERT, and Llama.
